About Me

My name is Sophia, I am born and raised in the South Bay area of California. Animation and illustration has been my passion since I was in elementary school. My artwork is heavily inspired by the pop culture I absorbed from my childhood growing up watching anime and Cartoon Network. I also find aesthetic inspiration from the low-poly nostalgic look of games from the Nintendo 64! I would like to consider myself a digitally literate person who can surf the web and keep up with the mainstream of internet culture and technology. Yet growing up on the internet did not prepare me for the art of creating digital media art or coding. Over this past semester I worked on expanding my knowledge in CSS and html, experimenting with videography through Adobe PremierePro and After Effects. I learned that coding is not as simple as it appears to be. The process is more extensive than copying and pasting, especially when I had a particular design in mind, which is why I feel comfortable finding inspiration and capturing small details. In Quar-ad-venture ( 2020) and Stay Home (Demo) (2020), the narratives and aesthetic choices were inspired by online web comics and video games from the late 90s/early 2000s. I want to pay homage to these interests that paved the way for technological and pop cultural development. Both of these works emulate adventures that fantasize normal life pre-COVID19, a lifestyle that realistically reflected my own. I was not interested in doing high-energy activities in the first place, now limited shopping and simply existing outside are adventures unfamiliar for me. One of the early projects I worked on, Transience: Capturing the Subtle Things in Life (2020), is reminiscent of the simplicity from Quar-ad-venture (2020) and Stay Home (Demo) (2020) in the way that the footage is taken from a transient and observational perspective. The colors chosen for the filters convey a playful and somewhat nostalgic feeling. My belief that my journey as an artist branches out and has the bandwidth to roll back into being inspired by the past. I enjoy timelessness, and seeing how technology and pop culture evolve along my journey.
