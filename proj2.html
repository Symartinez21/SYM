Spherical Magic Spill

Date: 3/16/20 Medium: TouchDesign, glass

video 1280x1280

My project Spherical Magic Spill follows a similar theme to the video collage I created for the last assignment. I enjoy the colors and overall aesthetic of citypop and “vintage” 80s-90s anime, which lead me to be inspired by Sailor Moon . In the concept art I drew, I initially wanted to bring more focus to the Sailor Moon theme and project onto a small blank white mirror with a silhouette of a rabbit. But I believed a crystal ball would be a stronger and more interesting installation. One of the first villains in the series wields her own crystal ball that gives her powers and allows her to oversee her enemies. When I learned that artists use projectors for their installations, that reminded me of Halloween decorations that use crystal balls. Fancier decorations will project a smaller image within the ball of a face, but I understand achieving that would require more engineering skills that I am not familiar with. So for this project I thought that attempting to emulate light in a dazzling way was more achievable for me through the crystal ball. Using a rainbow color palette inspired by vintage Japanese shoujo anime, I intended to create imagery that was reminiscent of the inside of a kaleidoscope. However I found Touchdesigner complicated and eventually followed a tutorial on particle displacement, which I thought was along the lines of my artistic vision for this project. For my materials I went to Dollar Tree and picked up a glass bowl (and broke it on the way home) and gathered small transparent gems (materials pictured below). Initially I was going to place the bowl with the gems inside and project through my materials using the school projector, but due to the coronavirus affecting my ability to enter campus, I had to improvise. I would set up my installation in a dark environment and use my Macbook screen to display the video I created on Touchdesigner. Then I would place the bowl on top of the screen and place the installation against a wall so that the projected image can be seen on a larger scale.
